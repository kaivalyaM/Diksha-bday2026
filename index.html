Day 3 — Our Ridiculous Story 💬

Okay Diksha. Sit down. Story time. No filter.

Same school. You were junior. We followed each other on Instagram — the way you follow 500 people and forget about 499 of them. That’s all it was. Or so we thought.

But here’s something I noticed only later — every single time the universe tried to push us together, there was a beach involved.

Beach #1 — Guhagar. School days. You posted a story from a trip with your Tai and Jiju. I replied. You replied. 2–3 messages… and then nothing. Two strangers who almost had a conversation.

Beach #2 — Goa. A couple years later, I posted about water sports. You replied. We talked again — 2–3 messages. You even asked me “do you know me?” And my very smooth answer was: “yes, same school. That’s what I know about you.” Incredible charm. Truly.

Two almost-conversations. Both at beaches. Both going nowhere. The universe, apparently, was being patient.

In between all this, I also very maturely unfollowed you at some point. For “some reasons.” I will not be explaining those today. Moving on.

Beach #3 — Kerala. I was near the coast. You liked my photo. One like. I saw I wasn’t following you, sent the request and a “Hi” within 30 seconds. Very calm. Totally not desperate. And this time — we didn’t stop talking. Hours on the way back. I didn’t want it to end.

2–3 months of talking every single day. And then I confessed. Bold move, very brave, 10/10 from me.

And you? You went quiet. Late replies. One word answers. I was out here checking my phone every 10 minutes like “delivered… delivered… still delivered…”

Here’s something I want you to know — I saw everything you were going through. Final year, health stuff, a rough patch that would have broken most people. I knew. And I chose to stay anyway. Not to impress you. Just because leaving felt wrong.

Apparently that made me a “green forest.” Your words, not mine. I will accept this title with full pride. Framing it. Putting it on a wall.

But even the greenest forest has a quiet moment where it wonders — is anyone even noticing? So one day, almost without thinking, I mentioned I might be moving to Gujarat for a new job.

And something shifted. You asked questions. You got concerned. The distance that had quietly grown between us — it disappeared in that one conversation. That’s when I understood. You weren’t uninterested. You were just scared. And honestly — so was I.

You were fighting your own feelings the whole time, and I was too stubborn — or maybe too hopeful — to walk away. And somewhere in that mess of almost-giving-up and almost-letting-go, you finally said yes.

I’m really glad one of us did.

And that beach — Guhagar — where it all quietly started with a story reply? We went back together. This time not as two strangers exchanging 2–3 messages, but as us. That trip is one of my favourite memories and you know it.

1 year and 3 months later — here we are. And I would unfollow you and follow you back a hundred times if it meant ending up exactly here.

— The boy who never moved to Gujarat
